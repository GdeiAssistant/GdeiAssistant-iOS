# Public Content Guidelines

## Purpose

Public-facing copy should be written for real users, not for AI tools, maintainers, or reviewers.

## What belongs in public pages

- User rights and obligations
- Privacy and sensitive-data notices
- Campus credential consent and management explanations
- Non-official-service disclaimers
- Marketplace, delivery, express, and lost-and-found risk reminders
- iOS permission explanations when relevant
- Complaint, reporting, and account-safety guidance

## What must not appear in public pages

- AI, Codex, or ChatGPT drafting notes
- TODO items or implementation placeholders
- Internal implementation instructions
- "Write this if..." or similar drafting rules
- Unverified legal entities, contacts, or filing information
- Unrelated framework documentation links
- Excessive policy navigation link lists
- Debug, mock, or test-account technical phrasing shown to ordinary users

## README style

- Keep the README concise and developer-oriented.
- Do not duplicate full policy text in the README.
- Do not overload the README with policy navigation links.
- Do not publish real or realistic test credentials in the README.
- Keep user-facing copy standards in app pages, not in the README.

## Policy date handling

- Do not change policy dates for editorial cleanup.
- Only update dates for substantive changes to user rights, obligations, privacy processing, dispute handling, or service rules.

## Review checklist

- No internal notes remain in public copy
- No AI-style drafting language remains in public copy
- No unverified legal or contact information was added
- Necessary user risk notices remain visible
- No unrelated framework links remain in public-facing guidance
- No unnecessary date changes were made
- No local build artifacts, certificates, or signing files were added
